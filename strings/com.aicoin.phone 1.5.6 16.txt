The upper band and lower band constitutes a path and under most circumstances the price trades inside this path. A break above the upper band represents overbought condition, while a break below the lower band represents oversold condition. The width of this path constantly changes as price volatility changes: when volatility is high it widens, and when volatility is low it narrows. 

BOLL Calculation: 
MID=MA(CLOSE, PERIOD) 
UPPER=MID + TIMES*STD(CLOSE, PERIOD) 
LOWER=MID - TIMES*STD(CLOSE, PERIOD) 

BOLL Interpretation:

Under such premises if the price passes upward through the upper band, a short-term decline will follow and this is a sell signal for day traders. If the price passes downward through the lower band, a short-term bounce will occur and this is a buy signal for day traders. 
2. When the path moves towards upper right or lower right instead of moving forward horizontally, it is considered in an abnormal condition. If the price passes upward through the upper band, an uptrend is likely to follow. If the price passes downward through the lower band, on the contrary, a downtrend is likely to follow.

VMA Interpretation: 
1. Buy signal: When price starts to consolidate after a long-term decline, trade volume tends to be low with 5-day VMA and 10-day VMA moving close to each other, and single-day trade volumes stay under 5-day VMA and 10-day VMA under most circumstances. 2. Hold signal: When price starts to move upwards from a swing low, trade volume increases under most circumstances and stays above 5-day VMA and 10-day VMA with 5-day VMA under most circumstances staying above 10-day VMA and moving upwards, this means that capital is pouring in and price uptrend will continue, and is therefore a signal to hold positions. 
3. Divergence signal: When uptrend action comes to an end, price achieves new all-time highs but action of price and VMA diverges (which includes trade volume moving below VMA, VMA flattening out or moving downwards), this is a signal that the chasing high desire of the the market has started to change and price may start to fluctuate, and it suggests that traders should be prepared to sell the positions. There may also be another situation: price constantly declines to new all-time lows but VMA flattens out with hints of surging, this indicates that buying force is strong and price is not far away from the bottom. Traders can wait and get in when the decline halts. 
4. Sell signal: When price rises continuously but trade volume fails to keep in pace for new record highs, instead becomes lower than 5-day VMA and 10-day VMA, while 5-day VMA turns from uptrend to downtrend, or passes downward through 10-day VMA, then this is a good sell signal. When choosing selling point please be aware: sometimes price starts to decline as early as when the gap between 5-day VMA and 10-day VMA starts to narrow, but sometimes price does not decline until 5-day VMA passes downward through 10-day VMA. 
If, while the price is surging, 5-day VMA passes downward through 10-day VMA and diverges from the cross point of previous time, then this is a even more reliable sell signal. 
5. Short signal: When price declines after reaching its peak, 5-day VMA and 10-day VMA move downwards with single-day trade volume breaking above them from time, to time, this is a signal of short positions and traders should be cautious before buying in. If 5-day VMA stays below 10-day VMA with price falling simultaneously, and 5-day VMA shows no signs to reverse from downtrend to uptrend, this often means that downtrend is likely to continue.
